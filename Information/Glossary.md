# Glossary

### Album: 
A release with 2 or more tracks included6
### Artist: 
An individual or group credited as the creator of a recording
### C line: 
Indicates copyright in creative works other than the sound recordings such as the cover art or other material included with the album
### Distributor: 
The company that delivers music from the artist or their record label to different platforms
### Genre: 
A classification of music characterised by a particular form or style, this can be very broad or very specific 
### Independent Artist: 
An artist that has self-released their own music without the aid of any Major label, not to be confused with indie which is a genre of music
### Independent Record Label: 
A Label that releases music without any affiliation  to a Major Label
### ISRC:
Unique Alphanumeric code given to every track to identify it
### Lossless: 
Music that is streamed or downloaded in a format that preserves every detail of the original recording
### Major Label: 
A major label is any label with a significant market share usually being Universal Music Group, Sony Music and Warner Music Group.
### Metadata: 
A collection of information embedded into a digital music file that contains information about the album or track such as track title, artist, and album name
### P line: 
Indicates the legal rights holder for a sound recording(Master)
### Publisher: 
Provides support for songwriters and composers to manage protect and monetise their compositions(the songs, not specific recordings)
### Record Label: 
Supports the artists in the creation distribution marketing and monetisation of their recordings(the audio itself)
### Rightsholder: 
An individual or entity that owns or controls the legal rights to a musical work
### Territory: 
A geographical area that defines where specific music rights and licenses apply.
